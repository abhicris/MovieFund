# Investor Deck Guide - MovieFund

## Overview

This guide provides a comprehensive structure and content outline for creating an investor pitch deck for MovieFund. Use this to create your presentation on Gamma (or PowerPoint/Keynote).

**Target Audience**: Seed round investors (angels, VCs, family offices)
**Deck Length**: 12-15 slides
**Presentation Time**: 10-15 minutes

### Slide 2: The Problem
**Content:**
- **Headline**: "Film Investment is Inaccessible"
- **Key Points:**
  - Film investment traditionally limited to studios, distributors, and ultra-high-net-worth individuals
  - Average investors have no access to movie investment opportunities
  - Production companies struggle to raise capital quickly
  - High barriers to entry (minimum investments, exclusivity)
  - Lack of transparency in film finance

**Visual:**
- Statistics on film industry size
- Comparison: traditional vs. accessible investment

  - Transparent process and returns
  - Diversified portfolio opportunities

### Slide 5: Business Model
**Content:**
- **Headline**: "Multiple Revenue Streams"
- **Revenue Sources:**
  1. **Structuring Fee**: 3-7% of capital raised per movie
  2. **Management Fee**: 1-2% of assets under management (AUM) annually
  3. **Transaction Fee**: 0.5-1% per investment
  4. **Performance Fee**: 10-20% of returns above hurdle rate (optional)

- **Unit Economics:**
  - Average movie: $5M budget
  - Average raise: $2M (40% of budget)
  - Structuring fee: $60K-$140K per movie
  - Management fee: $20K-$40K annually per $2M
  - Target: 20-30 movies/year = $2M-$5M revenue

**Visual:**
- Revenue model diagram
- Unit economics table

## Design Guidelines

### Visual Style
- **Colors**: Professional, movie-themed (dark blues, golds)
- **Typography**: Clean, modern fonts
- **Imagery**: High-quality movie stills, platform screenshots
- **Charts**: Clear, data-driven visuals

### Best Practices
- Keep slides simple and focused
- One key message per slide
- Use visuals to support, not distract
- Practice timing (10-15 minutes)
- Prepare for Q&A

## Q&A Preparation

### Common Questions

**1. How do you ensure movie quality?**
- Rigorous due diligence process
- Track record requirements
- Investment committee review
- Risk assessment

**2. What if a movie fails?**
- Diversification across multiple movies
- Risk disclosure to investors
- No guarantee of returns
- Learnings and improvements

**3. How do you compete with traditional financing?**
- Lower barriers to entry
- Faster process
- Transparent platform
- Access to new investor base

**4. What's your moat?**
- First-mover advantage
- Technology platform
- Network effects (investors + producers)
- Data and insights

**5. Regulatory concerns?**
- Working with legal counsel
- Compliance framework
- KYC/AML processes
- Appropriate licenses
